What is the design on Bicycle playing cards?

Design. Bicycle cards follow the pattern of the French deck, containing 52 cards (13 in each of two red and two black suits), and include two jokers. The Bicycle trademark is printed on the ace of spades. Current decks contain two information/instruction cards as well.

What type of cards are Bicycle cards?

Bicycle playing cards are a standard 52-card deck of red and black colored cards. each of the cards may have one of the four suits: spades, clubs, diamonds, and hearts. The numbers on the cards range form 2 to 10, then proceed onto “Jack.” The Bicycle trademark is typically printed on the Ace of Spades.

Why do they call them Bicycle cards?

Back in 1885, when the Russell and Morgan Printing Company was preparing to launch a new line of playing cards, the company asked its employees to suggest a catchy, memorable brand name. The winning suggestion was the name of the popular item that was then sweeping the nation: the bicycle.

What material is used for playing cards?

A playing card is a piece of specially prepared card stock, heavy paper, thin cardboard, plastic-coated paper, cotton-paper blend, or thin plastic that is marked with distinguishing motifs. Often the front (face) and back of each card has a finish to make handling easier.

What thickness are playing cards?

Typical playing card dimensions- Standard playing card decks are 2.5 inches by 3.5 inches (64mm X 89mm) and 0.17mm and 0.24mm thick. Bridge size playing cards come in at 2.25 inches by 3.5 inches (57mm X 89mm). Most cards have a weight of 280gsm (grams per square meter).

What gram is card stock?

Because of the difference in the way text- and card-stock pound weight is determined, a sheet of 65# card stock is thicker and heavier than a sheet of 80# text. The weight of cardstock ranges from 50# to 110# (about 135 to 300 g/m2).

What does Standard Index mean for playing cards?

This index refers to the size of the numbers on the picture of the cards. There are a number of different kinds of index available, but the most common choices are between Jumbo and Regular (or Standard) index. Regular index cards are the baseline that other cards are measured by.

How many GSM are playing cards?

Cardstock weight is measured in GSM (grams per square meter). This is the universal measure for playing card paper weight. The higher the number (300gsm, 330gsm, 350gsm), the thicker and heavier the stock and card will be. Generally speaking, heavier cardstock gives your cards a higher quality feel.

What is the standard size for playing cards?

3.5″ x 2.5″ Each deck is wide (poker) size with a standard index. The cards measure 3.5″ x 2.5″. The cards are made from plastic-coated paper, which makes them durable and easy to clean.

What are the 4 types of playing cards?

Composition. A standard 52-card deck comprises 13 ranks in each of the four French suits: clubs (♣), diamonds (♦), hearts (♥) and spades (♠), with reversible (double-headed) court cards (face cards).

What size cards do casinos use?

A bridge-size card measures 2 ¼ inches by 3 ½ inches while a poker-size card measures 2 ½ inches by 3 ½ inches. Most casinos in the United States use bridge-size cards in table games like Texas Hold’em because they typically are a little easier to shuffle and handle.
