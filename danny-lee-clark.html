Danny Lee Clark: Where Is He Now?

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Tuesday, July 23, 2024

The iconic 1989 competitive reality show “American Gladiators” was the subject of the Netflix documentary “Muscles & Mayhem,” which shows how the programme came to be. But despite ‘American Gladiators’ great audience during its existence, things weren’t all roses behind the scenes, as the programme had to deal with rumours regarding contrived challenges and drug addiction. The Netflix series introduces us to a number of fascinating characters, including former Gladiator Danny Lee Clark, better known by his stage name Nitro, since it discloses the truth through one-on-one interviews. So, let’s examine Danny’s life and see where he is right now, shall we?

Who Is Danny Lee Clark?

Danny’s family migrated to the United States when he was still a young child, despite the fact that he was born in the City of Zama, which is situated in the Kanagawa Prefecture of Japan. Additionally, readers will find it interesting to learn that Danny did not start participating in athletics until his time in college, which is also when he discovered his love for football. The former bodybuilder played football for San Jose State University, where he started his career as a star defensive lineman. Danny was also incredibly committed to the game, thus it took him little time to become a star player. After joining the Los Angeles Rams, he even had the chance to play in the National Football League.

Later, Danny also participated in the Professional European League, but at this time, he was just beginning to fall in love with bodybuilding. In fact, Danny’s football career benefited greatly from weightlifting and muscle development, which is how he initially became interested in bodybuilding. Danny, a bodybuilder by trade, debuted as Nitro, his stage moniker, in 1989 when he joined the cast of “American Gladiators.” Additionally, although leaving the show after serving as a part of the original cast from 1989 to 1992, Danny returned as Nitro between 1994 and 1995 before switching to an analyst and co-host role in the final season of the show in 1996.

Additionally, Danny continued to work on “American Gladiators” as a coordinating producer for the show’s 2008 resurrection. Naturally, his participation in the programme catapulted him into the public eye and opened the door to a prosperous career in the entertainment business. Danny went on to feature in a plethora of other films and TV episodes after being well-known for his role as Nitro, such as “Death Becomes Her,” “Equilibrium,” “Walker, Texas Ranger,” “California Dreams,” and “The Brother’s Garcia.” Additionally, Danny created numerous other screenplays on the side while also having the chance to appear as a participant on a few reality shows. He also wrote, directed, and produced the film “Looking for Bruce.”

Where Is Danny Lee Clark Now?

Danny Lee Clark holds a black belt in Tae Kwon Do, according to sources, but he has also been involved with the Young Story Tellers programme since 2002. Additionally, in 2009, the former bodybuilder released his memoir, “Gladiator,” to much critical acclaim. In 2013, the entertainment star’s acute heart attack came as a horrifying shock to Danny and his loved ones. Nevertheless, he has since bounced back from the scare and even penned the autobiography “F Dying,” which details his life following the catastrophe.


While Danny currently appears to reside in Los Angeles, California and has a close relationship with his son Tyler Clark, he actually owns and operates Gladiator Rock’n Run, a company where the former bodybuilder creates obstacle courses for amateur athletes and the general public. In addition, he is recognised with founding the Ten Thousand Pounds programme, which educates kids about obesity. Additionally, according to sources, Danny also provides his skills as a motivational speaker. We wish Danny the best in the years to come.
