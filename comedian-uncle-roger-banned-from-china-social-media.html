Comedian Uncle Roger Banned From China Social Media 

Comedian Uncle Roger is recently banned from Chinese Social Media. A violation of pertinent laws and regulations was given as justification for the ban in the message on his Weibo account. 

A ban on his Chinese social media accounts has been placed on comedian Nigel Ng, better known by the stage name Uncle Roger, as part of what appears to be Beijing’s campaign against comedians. 

Over the weekend, Ng’s Weibo and Bilibili accounts, China’s equivalents of Twitter and YouTube, were frozen.

This response came after Ng released a teaser for his upcoming show that ironically mocked China’s autocratic regime and made light of the risk of cancellation. The Malaysian comedian, who is residing in London, still needs to address the issue.

Recently, Chinese comedian Li Haoshi was detained and punished for joking about his dogs to a military rallying cry. 

Ng’s suspension occurred when Beijing implemented severe restrictions on political satire and displayed increased sensitivity toward humor. 

His whole stand-up show, which will highlight China’s surveillance regime and voice his concerns about possible fallout from the Chinese Communist Party, was supposed to be released soon before the suspension took place.

Shortly after publishing recordings from his live performance, where he warned that his sketches on Beijing’s strictly regulated politics and Chinese leader Xi Jinping would land him in trouble, Ng was banned from China’s Twitter-like platform Weibo.
