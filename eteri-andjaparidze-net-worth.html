Who is Eteri Andjaparidze?

Eteri Andjaparidze is a distinguished Georgian-American pianist and music professor known for her multifaceted artistry and insightful teaching. She has performed globally and is recognized for her contributions to piano pedagogy and performance.

What is her background and early life?

She was born on September 15, 1956, in Tbilisi, Georgia, into a musical family. Her music education began under her mother, Yvetta Bachtadze, and continued at the Moscow State Tchaikovsky Conservatoire.

She has won prestigious awards like the Grand Prix at the Montreal International Piano Competition and has been a laureate at the Tchaikovsky International Competition. Her performances have spanned major global venues with various esteemed orchestras.

Which educational institutions has she been associated with?

She has taught at institutions such as NYU Steinhardt, Mannes School of Music, DePaul University, and conservatories in Moscow and Tbilisi. She has also served as a visiting professor at other institutions worldwide, contributing significantly to music education.
