Raoul Heertje Full Overview and Wiki

The Pioneer of Dutch Stand-up Comedy

His career is a fascinating journey through the realms of comedy, television, and writing, each phase significantly contributing to his net worth and popularity.

Born on March 11, 1963, in Bussum, Netherlands, Heertje had a vibrant educational background, studying International Relations and Theatre Studies at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem before continuing his studies in Birmingham.

This unique blend of education paved the way for his distinct approach to comedy, integrating cultural insights with humor.

The Genesis of Comedytrain

In 1990, Raoul, alongside his brother Eric, founded Comedytrain, the first stand-up comedy collective in the Netherlands.

This initiative was revolutionary, introducing a new form of entertainment to the Dutch audience.

Comedytrain’s home theatre, Toomler, became the cradle of Dutch stand-up comedy, nurturing talents who would go on to dominate the comedy scene in the Netherlands.

This venture significantly boosted Heertje’s financial standing and established him as a cornerstone of Dutch comedy.

Rising to International Fame

His charm and wit transcended borders, leading to appearances on international TV shows hosted by personalities like Ruby Wax, Clive James, and Jeremy Clarkson.
