Who Is Andrew Pritchard?

Andrew Pritchard is now known as a humble man, but his past is full of crimes and drug smuggling stories. He served jail time and was acquitted in 2006.

Andrew started selling weed at home parties at the age of 14 before managing some of the biggest warehouse raves of the acid-house era.

Money began to flow in, but with it came difficulty, and he was forced to flee to Jamaica after someone was murdered at one of his parties.

Pritchard learned the smuggling trade's tactics, and with corrupt UK Customs officers in his pocket, nothing seemed to go wrong.

However, someone in his network then began moving large volumes of cocaine through his supply chain.

He was apprehended by a Customs task force when he met a counterfeit cigars shipment, which turned out to be half a ton of premium-grade cocaine.

Pritchard was acquitted after eighteen months of detention following two contentious trials.

Is Drugs Kingpin Andrew Pritchard Still In Jail Now?

Formally known as Drug Kingpin, Andrew Pritchard got released in 2006 after serving 18 months in prison. He is involved in humanitarian work and found his passion in the music industry. 

As a successful entrepreneur in the music and events industry, Andrew has created several live music concerts and large-scale music festivals.

He was also a highly successful drug smuggler, a category 'A' criminal with a lavish lifestyle until it all came crashing down, destroying his life and, more significantly, the lives of his family.
