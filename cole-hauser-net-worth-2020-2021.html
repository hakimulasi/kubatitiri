Early Life & Biography

Cole Hauser was born on 22nd March 1975 in Santa Barbara, California. His parents are Cass Warner and Wings Hauser. His father was an actor while his mother was the founder of Warner Sisters. His parents divorced when he was only two years and was raised by his mother. He reunited with his father when he was fifteen years and taught him about auditioning. Hauser did not complete his high school education as he decided to leave and pursue an acting career.

Personal Life

Hauser married his lovely wife, Cynthia Daniel, a former actress and photographer in 2006. Together, the couple has three children, two sons, Ryland and Colt Hauser, and one daughter Steely Rose. In 2016, Hauser found himself on the wrong side of the law after he was caught driving under the influence of alcohol. He was charged to serve ten days of community work, go for nine-month alcohol education, and also 36 months’ probation.

Career

Hauser realized his talent in acting when he was in high school when he took a role in Dark of the Moon play. He played his first role in a film, School Ties in 1992 alongside several other young actors like Brendan Fraser, Matt Damon, and Ben Affleck. In 1993, he starred another major role in the film Dazed and Confused as Benny O’Donnell, which he starred together with Ben Affleck. He participated in the 1995 film, Higher Learning as Scott Moss, the campus leader. In 1997 he appeared in another film, Good Will Hunting, together with Matt Damon and Ben Affleck, which reunited them.

Some of the other major films that he appeared in the subsequent years include Hart’s War in 2002, Tears of the Sun, and 2 Fast 2 Furious in 2003, The Break-Up in 2006, the Stone Angel in 2007, and A Good Day to Die Hard. In 2018, he filmed Acts of Violence and Running with the Devil in 2019. Hauser has appeared in various television debut, including A Matter of Justice, K-Ville, The Tower, Chase, and he is currently working on the drama series, Yellowstone, since 2018.

Awards & Achievements

Hauser received a nomination from Independent Spirit Award in 2001 as the Best Supporting Male Actor. In 2003, he won an award with Young Hollywood Awards for Breakthrough Performance in the male category.
