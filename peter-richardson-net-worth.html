Peter Richardson Overview and Wiki

Early Beginnings and Education

Peter Richardson, born on October 15, 1951, in Newton Abbot, Devon, England, began his journey in the entertainment world as a teenager. He first appeared in Alan Bennett’s play Forty Years On before honing his craft at the Bristol Old Vic Theatre School from 1971 to 1973.

His early years were marked by a blend of acting and creating experimental theatre shows, often collaborating with Nigel Planer. These shows, notably Rank and The Wild Boys, combined comedy with rock music and toured nationally.

The Comic Strip and Rise to Fame

Richardson’s most significant contribution to British comedy began with the founding of The Comic Strip, a group of performers that became a cornerstone of alternative comedy in the UK.

Alongside Planer, he formed a double act that became part of this troupe, which included future stars like French and Saunders, Rik Mayall, Adrian Edmondson, and Alexei Sayle.

The Comic Strip was pivotal in Richardson’s career, leading to the creation of The Comic Strip Presents…, a series of short, self-contained comedy films that aired on Channel 4. Richardson played multiple roles in this series, not just as an actor but also as a writer and director.

Filmography and Direction

His venture into film includes directing and writing several notable works. His films, such as The Supergrass, Eat the Rich, The Pope Must Die, and Churchill: The Hollywood Years, showcase his unique style, blending black comedy, parody, and satire.

Later Career and Contributions

In the 1990s, Richardson continued to innovate with the mockumentary comedy series Stella Street, co-written with Phil Cornwell and John Sessions. He also introduced a new generation of performers in his productions.

In 2004, he co-founded the production company Great Western Features in Totnes, Devon, which produced several films and commercials. His work in the 2010s includes writing and directing more Comic Strip films, such as The Hunt for Tony Blair, Five Go To Rehab, and Red Top.

Personal Life and Legacy

Peter Richardson’s personal life is marked by his marriage to Marta Richardson since 1981, with whom he has four children, including stand-up comedian Red Richardson. He describes himself as a Devonian and has set several of his films in Devon.
