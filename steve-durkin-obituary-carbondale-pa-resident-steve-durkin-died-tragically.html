Steve Durkin Obituary

Remembering the Life and Legacy of Steve Durkin

It is with heavy hearts that we announce the passing of Steve Durkin, a beloved member of the Horseheads and Painted Post communities. On March 31, 2021, Steve left us at the age of 56, leaving behind a profound impact on those who knew him. Today, we gather to celebrate his life, his accomplishments, and the memories we shared with him.

Death and Personal Information

A Life Well-Lived and a Legacy to Cherish

Steve Durkin, a native of Horseheads, New York, peacefully passed away in Painted Post, surrounded by his loved ones. Throughout his life, Steve was known for his dedication, kindness, and unwavering love for his family and friends. He will be remembered for his vibrant spirit, his passion for golf and sports, and his remarkable talent in woodworking.

Steve’s impact extended beyond his personal life. He was a legendary figure in the history of wiffle ball, achieving an impressive batting average of .358 and earning the prestigious Gold Glove Award three times. His dedication and skill on the field were an inspiration to many.

While we mourn the loss of Steve, we also celebrate the joy he brought to our lives. His memory will forever live on in our hearts.

Family Background

A Loving Family and Lasting Connections

Steve Durkin was born on June 5, 1964, in Corning, New York, to John Durkin and Carolyn “Corky” Gauss Kennedy. He was the first child of this loving couple, who named him Steven. Growing up, Steve shared a close bond with his siblings, Paul, Susan, and Karen, and cherished the support and love of his parents.

Steve’s family was his rock, and he was a devoted father to his children, Christopher and Alexis. He instilled in them the values of love, perseverance, and the importance of family bonds. Steve’s legacy lives on through his children, who will carry his spirit and continue to make him proud.

Steve is also survived by his father, John Durkin, and his mother, Carolyn Kennedy, as well as his extended family of nieces, nephews, cousins, and beloved aunts and uncles. His presence will be deeply missed, but his memory will forever be cherished by all who had the privilege of knowing him.

Professional Life

A Career Filled with Dedication and Achievement

Steve Durkin’s professional journey was marked by dedication, hard work, and a commitment to excellence. Throughout his career, he made significant contributions and left a lasting impact on the company he served.

Employment at Corning Incorporated

A Pillar of the Corning Incorporated Community

For over 29 years, Steve Durkin dedicated his talents and expertise to Corning Incorporated, a company renowned for its innovation and technological advancements. As a valued member of the Corning family, Steve played a vital role in the company’s success.

During his tenure, Steve demonstrated exceptional skills and a strong work ethic, earning the respect and admiration of his colleagues. His commitment to excellence and attention to detail were evident in every project he undertook.

Steve’s contributions extended beyond his professional responsibilities. He was known for his willingness to lend a helping hand and his ability to foster a positive work environment. His warm personality and genuine care for others made him a beloved figure among his coworkers.

Steve’s legacy at Corning Incorporated will be remembered for years to come. His dedication, professionalism, and unwavering commitment to the company’s values have left an indelible mark on the organization.

Personal Interests and Achievements

A Life Filled with Passion and Accomplishments

Steve Durkin’s life was not only defined by his professional success, but also by his diverse range of personal interests and remarkable achievements. He pursued his passions with unwavering dedication, leaving a lasting impact on those around him.

Love for Golf and Sports

Avid Golfer and Sports Enthusiast

Steve had a deep love for golf and was often found on the green, honing his skills and enjoying the camaraderie of the sport. His passion for golf extended beyond the game itself; it was a way for him to connect with friends, relax, and appreciate the beauty of the outdoors. Steve’s enthusiasm for sports extended beyond golf, as he was an avid supporter of the Yankees and the Bills. He found joy in cheering on his favorite teams and sharing the excitement of the game with fellow fans.

Woodworking and Dedication to Family and Friends

A Master Craftsman and Cherished Companion

Steve’s talent extended beyond the realm of sports. He had a passion for woodworking and spent countless hours creating beautiful pieces with his skilled hands. Whether it was crafting furniture, intricate designs, or personalized gifts, Steve’s craftsmanship was a testament to his creativity and attention to detail.

However, Steve’s greatest joy came from spending time with his family and friends. He was a pillar of support and a source of unwavering love and care. Steve’s dedication to his loved ones was evident in the time he spent with them, creating lasting memories and cherishing every moment together. His warm presence and genuine kindness made him a cherished companion to all who knew him.

Legendary Wiffle Ball Player

A True Icon in the World of Wiffle Ball

Steve Durkin’s name will forever be etched in the history of wiffle ball. He was a legendary player, known for his exceptional skills and remarkable achievements on the field. With a batting average of .358, Steve’s prowess with the wiffle ball bat was unmatched. His talent and dedication earned him the prestigious Gold Glove Award three times, a testament to his exceptional fielding abilities.

Steve’s impact on the world of wiffle ball extended beyond his individual achievements. He inspired others with his passion for the game and served as a mentor to aspiring players. His love for wiffle ball brought joy to countless individuals and created a sense of camaraderie within the community.

Steve Durkin’s legacy as a legendary wiffle ball player will continue to inspire future generations, reminding us of the power of dedication, skill, and the love for the game.

Surviving Family Members

A Network of Love and Support

Although Steve Durkin may no longer be with us, his memory lives on through the love and support of his surviving family members. Together, they form a tight-knit network that cherishes the bond they shared with Steve and continues to honor his legacy.

Children and Parents

A Lasting Legacy of Love

Steve’s children, Christopher Durkin of Buffalo and Alexis Durkin of Painted Post, are a testament to his love and devotion as a father. He instilled in them the values of compassion, resilience, and the importance of family. Christopher and Alexis carry their father’s spirit with them, embracing life’s challenges with the same determination and love that Steve exemplified.

Steve’s parents, John Durkin and Carolyn Kennedy, were a constant source of support and guidance throughout his life. Their unwavering love and encouragement shaped Steve into the remarkable person he became. John and Carolyn continue to honor their son’s memory, finding solace in the cherished moments they shared together.

Siblings and Extended Family

A Tapestry of Love and Connection

Steve’s siblings, Paul Durkin, Susan Sonner, and Karen Mazzarese, were not only his brothers and sisters but also his closest confidants and lifelong friends. Together, they shared countless memories, laughter, and support. The bond they formed with Steve remains unbreakable, serving as a reminder of the enduring power of family.

Steve’s extended family, including nieces, nephews, and cousins, were an integral part of his life. Their presence brought joy and laughter to family gatherings, creating a sense of unity and belonging. Additionally, Steve’s aunts and uncles, including Chuck and Judy Gauss, Bill Gauss, and Mike and Coleene Durkin, provided unwavering love and support throughout his journey.

As Steve’s surviving family members come together to celebrate his life, they find solace in the memories they shared and the love that continues to bind them. Their collective strength and support serve as a testament to the enduring legacy of Steve Durkin.

Steven J. Durkin, a beloved resident of Painted Post, New York, passed away on March 31, 2021, at the age of 56.
